Slime mold mechanisms

Slime mold is a broad term describing organisms that grow selectively based on the available food. If there is no food, slime mould grows exploratively. If there is food, the explorative branches die and the branches toward the food increase their capacity. The CS slime mold metaphor can be be used to handle intractable optimisation problems. In CS there are two slime-mold metaphors, viz. pipeline-based (Japan school 1995-now) and particle based (UK school, 2005-now). The particle-based approach is conceptually much simpler and gives results that are as rewarding the pipeline-based approach. Recently (2014), the particle-model has been used to tackle the travelling salesman problem, with some success.

CS models of slime mold are instances of so-called massive multi-agent learning or, more in particular, stigmercy-based multi-agent learning. Thousants of particles (agents) influence each other by dropping pheromone and perceiving pheromone on a grid.
