Periodicity and chaos in evolutionary dynamics

Description

It is well known that all interesting symmetric 2×2 games like the prisoner's dilemma, the chicken game, battle of the sexes, and stag hunt, can be generated by payoff matrices of the form ((R, S), (T, P)) (reward, sucker, temptation, punishment) where R=1, P=0, -1 ≤ S ≤ 1, 0 ≤ T ≤ 2. In such cases evolutionary dynamics always converges to a fixed population ratio. Based on this information, it is often taken for granted that evolutionary dynamics behaves nicely for all payoff values, but this assumption is not true. For payoff values (S,T)∉[-1,1]×[0,2] the dynamic sometimes becomes unpredictable in the sense that it is difficult to say on the basis of the values of S and T, whether the process converges. In the article of Vilone et al. it is discussed how payoffs outside the conventional S×T rectangle may lead to periodic or chaotic behaviour. A characteristic of the latter is that tiny changes in S and T may lead to radically different dynamics.
