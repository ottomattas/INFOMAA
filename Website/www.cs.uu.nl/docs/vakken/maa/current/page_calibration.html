Prediction, postdiction, and calibration

Like fictitious play and Bayesian learning, calibration tries to predict the future based on past play. A sequence of binary forecasts is calibrated if in all periods where the occurrence of 1 is predicted with p is actually confirmed by the empirical frequency distribution. In 1997-1999 it was proven that for any desired degree of accuracy ε > 0 there exist random forecasting rules that are ε-calibrated for all paths of play. In this period is was also proven that if all players use a calibrated forecasting rule and play best responses, the empirical frequency of play converges to the set of correlated equilibria.
