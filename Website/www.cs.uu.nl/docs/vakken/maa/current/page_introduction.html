Literature

Key

  1. “Strategic Learning and its Limits” Peyton Young (2004). Ch. 1 [scholar, lib]
  2. “Learning and Teaching” Shoham (2009). Ch. 7 of Multi-agent Systems [scholar, lib], Sec. 7.1.

Support

Emergence of social conventions

  1. “The Evolution of Conventions” Peyton Young (1993) [scholar, lib]
  2. “On the Emergence of Social Conventions” Shoham & Tennenholtz (1997) [scholar, lib]

Game theory

  1. Y. Shoham and K. Leyton-Brown. Essentials of Game Theory: A Concise, Multidisciplinary Introduction. Morgan & Claypool Publishers, 2008 [book]. (Almost entirely included in Shoham and Leyton-Brown's book on multi-agent systems that is freely downloadable through masfoundations.org.)
  2. S.P. Hargreaves Heap & Y. Varoufakis (2004), Game theory: a critical text, Routledge [book].

Learning in games

The following two books are key publications in this area.

  1. H. Peyton Young (2004): Strategic Learning and it Limits, Oxford UP. [book].
  2. D. Fudenberg and D.K. Levine (1998), The Theory of Learning in Games, MIT Press. [book].

Differential games

  1. Isaacs (1975): Differential games: a mathematical theory with applications to Warfare and Pursuit, Control and Optimization [book]. Re-issued by Dover in 1999.
  2. Friedman (1971): Differential games [book]. The last chapter, Ch. 8 is on n-person games.
  3. Hájek (1975): Pursuit Games: An Introduction to the Theory and Applications of Differential Games of Pursuit and Evasion [book]. Resissued by Dover in 2008.
  4. H. Stalford et al. (1973): “Sufficiency conditions for Nash equilibria in N-person differential games” in: Topics in Differential Games, Elsevier [scholar].
  5. D. Fudenberg et al. (1988): “Open-Loop and Closed-Loop Equilibria in Dynamic Games with Many players,“ in J. of Economic Theory 44(1). pp. 1-18 [scholar].
